Peltiphyllum Peltatum Umbrella Plant D 1' - 4' Spread by Rhizome Wet unknown RW LN Apr - July Very young leaf stalks & flower stalks peeled & eaten fresh. Loses flavor cooked. Steam older stalks (bland & stringy) / reported from Benton County south. More common S. Oregon Cascades mid to low altitudes. Inhabits western Cascade stream banks sometimes even growing in fast moving stream. Found in mixed forests & Ponderosa forests. Great plumes of flowers followed by giant leaves 1' to 3' across. Petasites Frigibus - See D Palmate Sweet Coltsfoot D 6" - 4' Creeping Rhizome Colonize Rich Moist yes RW LN Feb - June Young leaves & possibly flower stalks contain a modest amount of Purrolizidine Alkaloids that cause permanent liver damage. Mature leaves said to have almost none. Roots (rhizomes) may have none of this alkaloid. Indian Tribes (some) ate young leaves & flower heads. Also, peeled young stalks cooked / rhizomes sometimes eaten if not used for medicine. Important salt substitute for some tribes. Leaves & stems are dried, burned & used as a seasoning rich in mineral salts. R FW-PI

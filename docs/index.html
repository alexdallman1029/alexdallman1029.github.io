Eucommia ulmoides Hardy Rubber Tree T D 40'-70' As wide or wider Many Softwood cuttings or stratified seed R / SDT LN Spring Young leaves edible as steamed or stir fry vegetable, tasty delicate flavor, also good for tea. Chinese make a tonic soup by mixing the bark with chicken or pork. Leaf tea is popular for boosting the immune system, maintaining normal blood pressure levels and strengthening legs and lower back. The bark contains the compound pinoresinal diglucoside, an agent that effectively lowers early stage high blood pressure. Bark also highly regarded to treat osteoporosis and osteoarthritis, slows and may even reverse joint degeneration, reputed to strengthen bones and ligaments (connective tissues in joints). Bark used for various other illnesses in traditional Chinese medicine. Research indicates that the leaves should be interchangeable medicinally with the bark due to their comparable chemistry. Leaves used in Chinese folk medicine as an anti-fungal to treat ringworm and ahtlete feet. Winged fruit (seed) yield 27% oil used for miscellaneous industrial purposes. Bark contains about 3% rubber b y dry weight. This solid latex, called gutta percha, is used to insulate electric cables, etc. but extraction is difficult. wood is used for furniture, fuel, etc. and tends to sucker when cut (coppice).
